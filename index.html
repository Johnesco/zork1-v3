Zork I — v3: Multimedia

Sound, Color, and Atmosphere

Play (with sound) Play in IF Hub Source Walkthrough

v3 intentionally diverges from the original, leaning into what Inform 7 and modern web technology do best. Sound and atmospheric CSS visuals transform the text into an immersive experience.

This is where Parchment replaces Quixe as the interpreter — the Emglken WASM engine enables native Glk sound channels, playing .ogg audio embedded in the .gblorb binary. The entire soundscape is driven from the story source via Glk sound channel commands.

Multimedia Features

  • Ambient audio — zone-based background music across 10 zones (forest, cave, house, water, rapids, loud, hades, mine, machinery, silence) with smooth crossfades between areas
  • Sound effects — 16 one-shot audio triggers for key game events: puzzle moments, creature encounters, and atmospheric touches
  • Native Glk sound — audio driven from story.ni via Glk sound channels, bundled as a .gblorb and played through Parchment’s Emglken WASM engine
  • CSS mood palettes — zone-based color theming with smooth Houdini @property transitions: forest greens, cave blues, warm house tones
  • CRT intro — green phosphor terminal aesthetic on startup, fading to the game theme after first input
  • Tree effects — falling leaves, canopy glow, and wind sway when climbing the tree
  • Egg flash — golden explosion with shockwave rings and sparks when placing the egg in the trophy case
  • Sword glow — blue screen-edge vignette when the sword detects danger
  • Platform themes — in the IF Hub player, toggle between the native multimedia overlay and 10 retro platform styles (MS-DOS, Apple II, Commodore 64, and more)
  • Inherits all v2 bug fixes and v1 content
